# Comparative Analysis — Classical Texts, Schools, and Regional Traditions

## 1. Overview
Cross-source comparative analysis of [[Bazi (八字)]] reveals that the five classical texts, major schools, and regional traditions do not form a unified system. They operate from different foundational premises, prescribe different methods for identical charts, and have diverged significantly across cultures. This page synthesises findings from classical text review, NotebookLM multi-source queries, and academic literature.

## 2. Origin & Historical Context

### The Xu Ziping Problem
The system is attributed to Xu Ziping (徐子平), but historians raise serious doubts about whether he was a historical person or a school-lineage claim:
- No text definitively written by Xu Ziping survives.
- The attribution is made by Xu Dasheng, a student/compiler, in the preface to [[渊海子平 Yuan Hai Zi Ping]] — a common pattern in Chinese technical traditions for establishing lineage authority.
- Historians Ho Peng Yoke (*Chinese Mathematical Astrology*, 2003) and Jeffrey Kotyk (*Buddhist Astrology and Astral Magic in the Tang Dynasty*, 2017) trace the system's intellectual roots to:
  - Han Dynasty correlative cosmology (Yin-Yang/Five Phases frameworks)
  - 8th–9th century Tang Dynasty astrological milieu
  - Significant influence from Indian Jyotisha and Iranian/Zoroastrian star-lore via Buddhist transmission

The "single genius inventor" narrative is a legitimating device, not verified history.

## 3. Core Definition
Comparative analysis is the systematic comparison of how different Bazi sources (texts, schools, regional traditions) treat the same core questions: How is a chart read? What is the [[เทพใช้งาน (用神)|Useful God]]? How are dynamic interactions weighted?

## 4. Classical Text Comparison

### Foundational Premises by Text

| Text | Core Premise | Useful God Method | Signature Contribution |
|------|-------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| [[子平真诠 Zi Ping Zhen Quan|*Zi Ping Zhen Quan*]] (ZPZQ) | Month Branch determines [[โครงสร้างดวงชะตา (格局)|Pattern (格局)]]; structure is primary | From Month Branch — Yong Shen must support or flow from the pattern | Systematic pattern taxonomy; "bible" of structural school |
| [[滴天髓 Di Tian Sui|*Di Tian Sui*]] (DTS) | Dynamic balance of all 8 characters; conditional rules throughout | Contextual — depends on [[ธาตุเจ้าชะตา (Day Master)|Day Master]] strength and elemental balance | Poetic conditional reasoning; "separates capable from excellent" |
| [[穷通宝鑑 Qiong Tong Bao Jian|*Qiong Tong Bao Jian*]] (QTBJ) | Season/climate is primary; Day Master needs "medicine" | Seasonal fire/water tables — climate adjustment overrides structure | Tiao Hou (調候) climate school; winter charts need fire regardless of pattern |
| [[渊海子平 Yuan Hai Zi Ping|*Yuan Hai Zi Ping*]] (YHZP) | Encyclopedic; first comprehensive text | Multiple coexisting methods | [[ดาวพิเศษ (神煞)|Shen Sha]] tables; historical gateway text |
| [[三命通会 San Ming Tong Hui|*San Ming Tong Hui*]] (SMTH) | Encyclopedic compilation | Pattern + Shen Sha hybrid | Primary Shen Sha source; Ming dynasty synthesis |

### The Useful God Contradiction (ZPZQ vs QTBJ)
The most practically significant contradiction: for a winter Jia Wood (甲木) Day Master with strong Water pattern, **ZPZQ** says support the Water pattern (Water is Yong Shen), while **QTBJ** says "medicine is fire" — introduce Fire to warm the chart even if it destroys the pattern structure. There is no agreed meta-rule for which school takes precedence. Practitioners who blend both systems routinely produce conflicting readings for identical charts.

See [[เทพใช้งาน (用神)]] for the full contradiction analysis.

## 6. Academic and Epistemological Critique

### Non-Falsifiability Problem
Bazi interpretation relies on polysemous language — the same element or [[สิบเทพ (十神)|Ten God]] can have multiple legitimate meanings. This enables post-hoc rationalization: any life outcome can be "explained" after the fact by selecting the appropriate meaning. No published study has tested Bazi predictions prospectively with pre-registered hypotheses.

### Empirical Research
The most rigorous empirical work touching Bazi:
- **Korean TCI-RS Study** (ResearchGate): Tested Ten Gods against Cloninger's Temperament and Character Inventory–Revised (TCI-RS) personality dimensions across Korean *Saju Palja* practitioners. Found statistically significant correlations for several Ten God categories. Limitations: self-selected practitioners, retrospective assignment, small N.
- **Ben Ming Nian investor behavior** (SSRN 2019, ScienceDirect 2020): Two behavioral economics studies on whether birth-year animal-sign beliefs affect investor risk tolerance. Empirically tractable because outcome (market behavior) is observable, not the system's predictions themselves.
- Western sinological scholarship (Ho Peng Yoke, Kotyk, Martzloff): Rich on history and textual lineage; does not address predictive validity.

### The Thai Academic Gap
Thai academic engagement with Bazi as a subject of study is near-zero. The Nova Masters school is the primary Thai institutional transmission point; no Thai university has published peer-reviewed Bazi research. Thai practitioners use standard Chinese classical methods with a Thai solar date converter.

## 8. Research Gaps and Common Misconceptions

**Research Gaps:**
- Prospective prediction testing (pre-registered, blind)
- Vietnam regional divergences (understudied)
- Systematic comparison of Mang Pai vs ZPZQ outcomes on identical chart sets
- Historical tracing of when and how QTBJ climate method entered mainstream practice
- Large-scale empirical study: standard time vs [[เวลาสุริยะจริง (True Solar Time)|True Solar Time]] — ผลลัพธ์ต่างกันอย่างมีนัยสำคัญหรือไม่

**Common Misconceptions:**
- ❌ "Xu Ziping invented Bazi" — contested; likely a school-legitimating myth
- ❌ "Classical texts agree on method" — they demonstrably do not on Useful God
- ❌ "Mang Pai is easier" — it is less systematic, not simpler
- ❌ "Korea/Japan just use Chinese Bazi" — both have developed significant independent traditions
