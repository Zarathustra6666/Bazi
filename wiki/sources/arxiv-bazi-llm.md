# Academic Papers: BaZi as LLM Benchmark (2025–2026)

> **Type:** academic paper (x2)
> **Date:** 2025-10-27 (2510.23337) · 2026-02-13 (2602.12889)
> **URLs:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.23337 · https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.12889

## Overview

Two independent research teams (2025–2026) have converged on BaZi as a rigorous testbed for evaluating LLM capabilities in symbolic and temporal reasoning. Both papers treat BaZi not as folklore but as a fixed, rule-governed system whose combinatorial complexity and multi-layer temporal structure make it an unusually clean benchmark domain. Taken together, they establish that (1) modern frontier LLMs can reason with BaZi knowledge above chance, (2) performance degrades severely on temporally composed or multi-condition queries, and (3) hybrid symbolic+LLM pipelines substantially outperform pure LLM prompting. For this vault, they are the first peer-reviewed operationalization of the conceptual vocabulary used throughout the wiki — [[ten-gods]], [[heavenly-stems]], [[earthly-branches]], [[shen-sha]], [[luck-pillar-arc]] — and provide quantified evidence of where that vocabulary is tractable for machines and where it remains hard.

## Paper 1 — BaZi-Based Character Simulation Benchmark (arXiv 2510.23337)

**Full title:** *BaZi-Based Character Simulation Benchmark: Evaluating AI on Temporal and Persona Reasoning*
**Authors:** Siyuan Zheng, Pai Liu, Xi Chen, Jizheng Dong, Sihan Jia
**Date:** 2025-10-27
**Venue:** WordPlay Workshop 2025

### Abstract
The authors introduce the first QA dataset for BaZi-based persona reasoning ("Celebrity 50") and a BaZi-LLM hybrid pipeline that integrates symbolic BaZi rule extraction with an LLM generation layer. The system accepts birth data (date, time, gender, location) and outputs a temporally dynamic persona profile across five life domains, which can be queried against real biographical records to evaluate accuracy.

### Methodology
The pipeline has four sequential stages:
1. **Input layer** — birth date, time, gender, birth latitude/longitude
2. **BaZi rule mapping** — symbolic feature extraction (Five Elements balance, stem/branch interactions)
3. **Symbolic interpretation** — [[ten-gods]], [[shen-sha]], Pattern Structures (格局); rules applied in order to derive trait and event predictions per domain
4. **Scenario-oriented generation** — LLM constructs dynamic persona narrative conditioned on the symbolic output; generates multiple-choice QA for evaluation

The benchmark dataset (Celebrity 50) covers 50 public figures from 29 countries (37 male, 13 female), born circa 1940, Northern Hemisphere. 488 QA pairs (~9.76 per person) across five domains. Ground-truth answers derived from verified biographical records. QA items generated by Qwen API then filtered to remove proper nouns, overly specific numbers, and questions exceeding "reasonable predictive capabilities" of BaZi analysis.

### Key findings
- The hybrid BaZi-LLM system achieves **30.3%–62.6% accuracy improvement** over vanilla frontier LLMs (DeepSeek-v3, GPT-5-Mini, Gemini-2.5-Flash)
- Shuffled-birthday control (randomizing birth-data→chart mapping): accuracy drops 20%–46%, confirming the system depends on genuine BaZi symbolic alignment, not surface text patterns
- DeepSeek-V3 real vs. shuffled: 51.2% → 40.6% (−20.7%); GPT-5-mini: 55.3% → 30.0% (−45.7%); Gemini-2.5-flash: 47.1% → 35.5% (−24.6%)

## Paper 2 — BaziQA-Benchmark (arXiv 2602.12889)

**Full title:** *BaziQA-Benchmark: Evaluating Symbolic and Temporally Compositional Reasoning in Large Language Models*
**Authors:** Jiangxi Chen, Qian Liu
**Date:** 2026-02-13

### Abstract
The authors construct a 200-question multiple-choice benchmark derived from professional problems used in the Global Fortune-teller Competition (2021–2025). Each instance supplies a pre-computed, consistently formatted natal chart and requires multi-step inference integrating static chart structure with decadal and annual luck cycles. The paper evaluates six frontier LLMs under standard prompting and under a novel Structured Reasoning Protocol (SRP).

### Methodology
- **Format:** 4-option MCQ, 25% random baseline; 200 problems (40/year × 5 years), 8 subjects × 5 questions per year; questions ordered by increasing inferential complexity within each subject block
- **Chart representation:** Heavenly Stems and Earthly Branches formatted as a consistent structured context (not raw birth date); [[ten-gods]] relations pre-computed; decadal (大运) and annual (流年) cycles explicit
- **SRP (Structured Reasoning Protocol):** three-stage inference scaffold: (1) quantitative chart assessment, (2) interaction severity ranking, (3) domain-specific symbol-to-outcome mapping
- **Evaluation:** macro-average accuracy across five competition years; domain-wise breakdown across eight life domains

### Key findings

**Overall accuracy (macro-average):**

| Model | Accuracy |
|---|---|
| DeepSeek-Chat-V3 | 36.7% |
| DeepSeek-R1 | 34.1% |
| GPT-5.1-Chat | 32.5% |
| Gemini-2.5-Flash | 32.4% |
| Gemini-3-Pro | 32.1% |

All models significantly exceeded the 25% baseline (p < 10⁻⁷).

**Domain performance:**
- Static domains (personality, family): 40–53% accuracy — LLMs handle chart-structural reasoning adequately
- Temporal domains (annual fortune, career): 23–40% — performance collapses when decadal + annual layers must be integrated simultaneously
- Questions requiring precise temporal localization or multiple simultaneous symbolic conditions: near-zero success across all models

**SRP effects:** mixed — modest gains in some domains, performance degradation in others; suggests reasoning-order sensitivity rather than a universal scaffold benefit

## Bazi Knowledge Formalization

Both papers operationalize the same core ontology, establishing an implicit shared standard:

| Component | Paper 1 (2510.23337) | Paper 2 (2602.12889) |
|---|---|---|
| [[heavenly-stems]] + [[earthly-branches]] | Input → symbolic features | Pre-computed chart context per subject |
| [[ten-gods]] (十神) | Explicit interpretive layer; "symbolic roles between Day Master and other stems/branches" | Symbolic interaction framework, pre-computed in chart |
| [[shen-sha]] (神煞) | Auxiliary markers for "specific life tendencies or external influences" | Not foregrounded |
| Pattern Structures (格局) | Higher-level groupings reflecting personality orientation | Not foregrounded |
| Decadal luck (大运) | Temporal persona dynamics | Core temporal layer; primary source of difficulty |
| Annual luck (流年) | Included in dynamic states | Primary source of difficulty, especially when combined with 大运 |
| Life domains | Wealth, health, kinship, career, relationship (5 domains) | Marriage, career, health, wealth, personality, family, education, annual fortune (8 domains) |
| Event classification | Life-event QA pairs grounded in biographical records | MCQ judgment conditioned on professional competition problems |

The convergence on [[ten-gods]] as the central symbolic layer is significant: both teams treat 十神 as the primary mediating structure between chart inputs and life-domain outputs — consistent with the classical [[bazi|Zi Ping]] tradition and with masters such as [[liang-xiangrun]] and the [[di-tian-sui]] framework.

## Implications for Vault

1. **Ten Gods as core API.** Both papers treat 十神 as the primary symbolic interface between chart and prediction. The vault's existing [[ten-gods]] page should be treated as load-bearing; gaps there propagate to any analytical chain.

2. **Temporal composition is the hard problem.** Paper 2 demonstrates empirically that integrating natal chart + 大运 + 流年 simultaneously is where both humans (competition difficulty) and LLMs (23–40% on temporal domains) struggle. The vault's [[luck-pillar-arc]] page (Section 7: Annual × LP Interaction Matrix) is directly relevant here.

3. **Hybrid symbolic+LLM outperforms pure LLM.** Paper 1's 30–62% accuracy gain from adding structured BaZi rules suggests that the explicit rule base maintained in this wiki has practical predictive value as a symbolic grounding layer.

4. **Celebrity 50 dataset limitation.** 50 Western figures born ~1940 is a narrow slice; the vault's case material (Chinese masters, classical cases) is culturally and temporally distinct. Findings about accuracy may not transfer directly.

5. **Open gap — Shen Sha quantification.** Paper 1 includes [[shen-sha]] as a layer but does not report domain-specific accuracy attributable to Shen Sha vs. Ten Gods alone. This is an unresolved question for the vault's [[shen-sha]] concept page.

## Empirical Notes

- Both papers confirm BaZi reasoning is **non-trivial for frontier LLMs** — significantly above chance but far from professional-grade performance
- Paper 2's hard ceiling (~37%) is consistent with the observation that professional BaZi reading requires integrative symbolic judgment that current architectures handle poorly
- Paper 1's shuffled-birthday control is methodologically strong: it isolates symbolic grounding from generic biographical pattern matching
- Acknowledged limitations:
  - Paper 1: LLM-generated (Qwen) QA introduces hallucination risk; 50-person dataset, gender-imbalanced; astro.com birth data may contain errors; Western/1940s bias limits generalizability
  - Paper 2: Domain labels derived from heuristic text analysis (coarse-grained); only 5 competition years; no task-specific fine-tuning; SRP is inference-time only (no parameter modification)
- Neither paper addresses the epistemological status of BaZi predictions (whether they are *true*); both treat the system as a formal symbolic domain and evaluate internal consistency of reasoning
