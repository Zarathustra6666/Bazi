# Trey Bazi — Classical BaZi and Contemporary ZWDS

> **Type:** blog  
> **Author(s):** Trey (pseudonym; self-described "farmer, not a master")  
> **Active:** 2013–2018  
> **URL:** http://treybazi.blogspot.com  
> **Accessed:** 2026-06-04

## Overview

An English-language blog by a practitioner who teaches himself Classical Chinese to read primary Bazi sources directly. The blog serves simultaneously as a personal learning record and a teaching resource for non–Chinese-literate enthusiasts.

The blog's central commitment is strict adherence to the **classical Zi Ping tradition** (子平法), specifically the **格局派 Structural Sect**, drawing from [[yuan-hai-zi-ping|Yuan Hai Zi Ping]] (YHZP), [[zi-ping-zhen-quan|Zi Ping Zhen Quan]] (ZPZQ), [[san-ming-tong-hui|San Ming Tong Hui]] (SMTH), and [[di-tian-sui|Di Tian Sui]] (DTS). Trey explicitly warns that classical and modern Bazi are "two distinguished methods" — irreconcilable systems that practitioners routinely and harmfully conflate. He subordinates all modern concepts (DayMaster-strength-first analysis, simplified god counting) to classical chart-structure logic anchored in the [[useful-god|Month Command]] (月令).

The canonical five texts Trey works from are: **ZPZQ, SFTK, SMTH, XPHH, YHZP**. (SFTK and XPHH identities unconfirmed from snippets alone; likely 神峰通考 and 星平会海 respectively — [unsourced].)

Trey also covers ZWDS (Zi Wei Dou Shu / 紫微斗数) as a parallel system, but the majority of substantive posts focus on Bazi structure, classical text translation, and case studies.

## Key Positions

**1. Classical and Modern Bazi are IRRECONCILABLE systems.**  
The two systems have "very different rules." Practitioners trained in modern Bazi will encounter "contradictions" in classical texts — these are not errors but genuine methodological divergence. Mixing the systems produces incoherent analysis.

**2. Month Command (月令 YueLing) is absolute.**  
Chart [[patterns|structure]] and [[useful-god|Useful God]] can ONLY be derived from the Month Branch (月令). Called "the Key Point" in almost all classical literature; its historical origin traces to the Book of Rites (礼记, ~200 BC). The sole exception is JianLu / YueJie, where the Month Branch carries no usable Useful God — only then may other pillars supply the structural anchor.

**3. Day Master strength is secondary, not primary.**  
Modern Bazi: assess DM strength first → derive Useful God from the imbalance.  
Classical Bazi: derive Pattern from Month EB first → DM strength matters only within the requirements of that Pattern. Trey explicitly recants his early celebrity chart analyses when moving to classical approach.

**4. 顺用 / 逆用 (Shun-yong / Ni-yong) is the central analytical axis.**  
Benevolent gods (Food/Wealth/Officer/Seal) → 顺用: use directly, protect them.  
Malevolent gods (7K/HO/YangBlade/RobWealth) → 逆用: control, transform, or tame. The structure dictates which method applies.

**5. Benevolent/malevolent god distinction is hard and categorical.**  
Classical: Direct Officer, Wealth, Eating God, Direct Seal = benevolent. Seven Killings, Hurting Officer, Yang Blade, Rob Wealth = malevolent. Malevolent gods can become auspicious when properly controlled — never simply by being present. Modern Bazi blurs or ignores this distinction.

**6. 情 (Affection) and 力 (Strength) both required.**  
A beneficial god must be both relationally correct for the chart (有情) AND have sufficient Qi to deliver results (有力). Either condition alone is insufficient for excellence.

**7. Earthly Branches are passive — activated only through Stems.**  
HS are dynamic (act, generate, counter, exhaust). EB are static (await activation). EB combinations matter only when they shift elemental balance at the stem level. Branch combinations do not mirror stem combination logic.

**8. JianLu and Month RobWealth are ONE structure.**  
Trey follows ZPZQ in collapsing the distinction. The practitioner's task in this structure is to find the useful god from whatever protrudes — Officer, Wealth, or Food/HO — not to treat the month position as itself the structure anchor.

**9. Follow Output (从儿格) ignores DM strength entirely.**  
Unique among Follow Patterns: DM strength is irrelevant. Only Output-Wealth cycle strength matters. This is the only classical structure where a weak DM is explicitly acceptable if Output dominates.

**10. True vs False (真假) gods — Month Command as anchor.**  
"True god attains usefulness → noble life. Use the unreal → merely commoner." True = holds genuine Month Command. Finding it requires skill; the poem acknowledges "the mixing of the true and the fake is difficult to analyze."

**11. Clear (清) vs Murky (浊) is a chart quality spectrum.**  
DTS teaching: completely clear → noble and wealthy; half-clear/half-murky → cyclical success and failure; completely murky → suffering. Quality dimension orthogonal to Pattern type.

**12. GuiKang (魁罡) is practically near-extinct.**  
Trey estimates fewer than 1% of theoretically qualifying charts meet the strict conditions. Month Command must be genuinely empty of useful structure AND no Wealth or Officer can appear anywhere in the chart.

**13. Useful God has 6 named subcategories.**  
顺用 (Follow Use), 逆用 (Against Use), 清浊 (Purity/Impurity), 救应 (Rescue Response), 忌神 (Taboo God), 相神 (Minister God). Structural analysis requires identifying all six dimensions, not just the primary Useful God.

## Raw Notes

### A. Classical vs Modern — 4 Structural Differences (2018)

**Difference 1 — Role of Day Master Strength**
- Modern: DM strength is the primary lens. Strong → favor Outputs/Wealth/Officer. Weak → favor Parallels/Seals.
- Classical: DM strength is secondary to chart structure. It matters only within the requirements of a given Pattern.

**Difference 2 — Derivation of Chart Structure and Useful God**
- Modern: Useful God derived from any stems/branches after checking DM strength against Month EB.
- Classical: "Chart structure and useful god can ONLY be derived from the Month EB." Exception: JianLu/YueJie where no standard useful god occupies the month position.

**Difference 3 — Benevolent/Malevolent God Distinction**
- Modern: De-emphasizes the categorical split; both types treated as context-dependent.
- Classical: Hard distinction — benevolent = Food/Wealth/Officer/Seal; malevolent = HO/7K/YangBlade/Rob Wealth. Each has its own operational rules.

**Difference 4 — 顺用 (Going With) vs 逆用 (Going Against)**
- Modern: Rarely invoked.
- Classical: Foundational. Benevolent gods → use directly. Malevolent gods → control, transform, or strategically abandon.

### B. Day Master Strength — 4-Factor Method (2013, self-critiqued)

Four factors the author used before moving to classical approach:

1. **Intensity (势)** — Born in-season = "timely." Wood/spring, Fire/summer, Metal/autumn, Water/winter. In-season guarantees monthly root support.
2. **Qi (气)** — 12 Qi stages. Xu Lewu: "Having one Storage is better than having one BiJian." Storage and residual Qi matter differently for Yang vs Yin DMs.
3. **Engendering and Exhausting (生泄)** — Many engendering elements ≠ strong DM. Overly strong engenderer + weak DM = 母旺子衰 (compassionate mother destroys child).
4. **Rooted Connection (通根)** — DM roots in HS or EB regardless of polarity.

**Self-critique:** Trey updates his celebrity chart assessments multiple times in the post (Lady Gaga, Oprah, Bill Gates, David Cameron). Conclusion: "determining DM strength remains largely subjective and may be less crucial than identifying the Useful God and overall chart structure." By 2014 ("Is my Daymaster strong or weak?") and 2018, he explicitly deprecates this as primary lens in favor of Pattern-first.

### C. Heavenly Stems vs Earthly Branches (ZPZQ translation, 2013)

Core principle from ZPZQ: "Stems primarily represent Heaven — it is moving and it acts. Branches primarily represent Earth — it is still and awaits use."

- **HS**: Single-element; actively engage, generate, counter, support, exhaust.
- **EB**: Multi-element (Hidden Stems); static; can punish/clash/break/harm only through specific interactions that eventually move at stem level.
- **Mixing rule**: Stem combinations (e.g., Geng-Yi) create inauspicious "mixing" of Officer-Devil. But Shen-You branches are NOT treated identically — branch combinations don't mirror stem combination logic.
- **Practical implication**: Branches influence predictions primarily by creating stem-level movement. Branches alone remain passive.

### D. Ten Gods — Classical Benevolent/Malevolent Framework (2014)

**Benevolent gods (顺用 — use directly, protect):**
- 正官 Direct Officer: "Leader of the 10 Gods." Must be protected at all costs; cannot coexist with 7K without harm.
- 财 Wealth (Direct + Indirect): Property and resources. Indirect Wealth (偏财) is "the more powerful of the 2" — a nuance often lost in simplified tables.
- 食神 Eating God: "Offspring of DM." Protects DM, generates Wealth. Symbolizes food/clothing/longevity/offspring. (Trey prefers "Food God" as translation — 食 Shi = food, as in 枭神夺食.)
- 正印 Direct Seal: Intelligence, wisdom, compassion, health, integrity, authority.

**Malevolent gods (逆用 — control, transform, tame):**
- 七杀 Seven Killings (7K): Most dangerous. Counters DM. Becomes auspicious when controlled by Food God or Seal → transforms to Indirect Officer.
- 伤官 Hurting Officer (HO): Injures Direct Officer but simultaneously generates Wealth for DM. Talent + pride/ego.
- 枭神 Owl God: "Seizes Food" — undermines blessings/longevity when Food is the useful god. When Food is NOT used, functions as regular Indirect Seal (can favor strong DMs).
- 阳刃 Yang Blade: Only for Yang DMs. Extreme RobWealth — clashes Direct Officer, robs Wealth, saves 7K. Requires Officer or 7K to subdue it.

**Key rule:** "Malevolent deities can become auspicious when properly used" — their influence depends on dynamic structural relationships, not inherent nature alone.

### E. Shun-yong / Ni-yong Operational Rules (ZPZQ)

From the 逆用 post:

- **7 Killings (七煞):** Likes to be controlled by Food God; dreads support from Wealth and Seal. → ni-yong via Food.
- **Hurting Officer (伤官):** Likes to be controlled by Seal; in certain structures, HO-generates-Wealth works. → ni-yong.
- **Yang Blade (阳刃):** Requires Officer or 7K to subdue. → ni-yong via Officer/7K.
- **Rob Month (月劫):** Benefits from protruding Officer or Wealth, with Food as transformer. → ni-yong via Officer/Wealth.

Trey emphasizes: "these structural patterns are not exhaustible" — the ZPZQ list is illustrative, not mechanical. Real analysis requires contextual judgment.

### F. JianLu / YueJie Structure (ZPZQ, 2018)

**Definition:** DM born in month containing same elemental quality as itself, with no other useful god in the month position.

**Elemental month pairings:**
- Wood (Jia/Yi): Yin or Mao months
- Fire (Bing/Ding): Si or Wu months
- Earth (Wu/Ji): Chen, Xu, Chou, Wei months
- Metal (Geng/Xin): Shen or You months
- Water (Ren/Gui): Hai, Zi, Chou months

**Core rule:** "JianLu and Month RobWealth are one structure" — no need to distinguish. When other elements protrude, identify the secondary useful god: Wealth, Officer, or Food/HO.

**Structure rankings within JianLu:**
- Officer structure: Requires Wealth or Seal support. "Solitary Officer without assistance is not good."
- Three Wonders (三奇, SanQi): Wealth + Officer + Seal with Officer between them = "especially noble Qi."
- Wealth structure: Needs Food/HO to mediate RobWealth-Wealth conflict.
- 7K structure: Must be restrained or transformed into Wealth via elemental combinations.
- Spring Wood + Autumn Metal using Food/HO = "especially refined" 顺用 ("wood meets fire is bright; metal engenders water is soulful").

### G. Follow Output (从儿格) — Six Criteria (DTS, 2018)

Classical DTS verse: "Once leaving the door, only the son is seen... Follow son does not matter if self is strong or weak... as long as my son also gets his son."
- "Door" = Month EB; "Son" = Output element; "Son's son" = Wealth element

**Six criteria:**
1. Month EB must be the Output element.
2. Output protrudes in Heavenly Stems.
3. Output has strongest Qi among all five elements.
4. Wealth element must be present in the chart.
5. DM strength is irrelevant if Output and Wealth are robust.
6. Output must be strong AND generate Wealth (productive cycle).

**Distinction from other Follow Patterns:** Follow Output explicitly sets aside DM strength — the only classical structure where weak DM is acceptable if the Output-Wealth cycle is dominant.

DTS classical verse: four-level quality spectrum.

| Level | Chart State | Life Outcome |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| Completely clear | "Essence and spirit" | Truly noble and wealthy |
| Murky running toward clarity | "Chilling valley returns to spring" | Fortune attained when timing aligns |
| Half clear, half murky | Mixed | Many successes and failures through days and nights |
| Completely murky | "Plate full of murky Qi" | Suffering and hardship |

Connects to Pattern Purity: Clear ≈ 純格/有情格; Murky ≈ 雜格. Quality dimension within any Pattern type.

### I. True and Fake (真假) Gods — DTS (2015)

Core verse: "The true god attains usefulness, one's life is noble. Use the unreal, one is merely a commoner."

- **True god**: Holds genuine Month Command (得令). Identifying and using it → nobility.
- **False god**: Appears prominent but lacks Month Command root. Using it → ordinary outcomes.
- **Difficulty**: "The mixing of the true and the fake is difficult to analyze."
- **Alternative method**: "In obscured places, look for the real — it is also attainable." When standard method fails, implicit god identification is still possible.

### J. The Original ZiPing — Shen Xiaozhan's Framework (2015)

Wan YuWu's commentary on Xu Ziping foundations:

**Philosophical basis:** "TaiJi is judged as Heaven and Earth, one Qi is separated as Yin and Yang."

**Two central principles:**
1. Day Stem as Master — all eight-character analysis coordinates around Day Stem.
2. Month Branch as Luck anchor — seasonal influence determines elemental rise and fall.

**Shen Xiaozhan's two evaluative criteria (情×力):**
- **情 (Affection):** Correct relational functioning — Officer protects Seal, Seal protects DM, etc.
- **力 (Strength):** Each entity must have sufficient Qi to actually function.

Charts require both for excellence. Maps directly to [[master-reading-synthesis|Master Reading Synthesis]] 情×力 framework.

**Day Master quality:** A "good" DM exhibits 建旺 (Healthy and Prosperous) — either receiving support when weak OR maintaining Qi when strong. Weakness is not inherently inferior if properly supported.

### K. Four Words Invincible Steps (四言独步 SYDB) — YHZP

Ancient poem found inside YHZP, predating Xu Ziping. Significant because it establishes Month Command priority as ancient principle, not a later innovation.

Key lines:
1. 先观月令，论格推详 — **"First observe the Month Command; then discuss structure in detail."** The primary methodological instruction.
2. 以日为主，专论财官 — "With Day Master as master, focus primarily on Wealth and Officer."
3. 四柱排定，三才次分；日干为主，配合元辰 — Four Pillars set, Three Talents subdivided (Heaven=stems, Earth=branches, Human=hidden stems); Day Stem as master, combined with Yuan Chen.

SYDB distinguishes "single" (clean, single-deity Month Command) from "messy" (multiple competing deities) chart structures. Precision in identifying whether elements combine away or counter away is foundational.

### L. Case Study — Warren Buffett (Indirect Seal Structure, 2018)

- DM: Ren Water (壬水); Month: Shen (申) — Metal month, strong Geng metal protruding
- Structure: Indirect Seal (偏印格)
- Wealth star: Ding fire (丁火) — functions as "minister god" (相神)
- Ding reinforced by Jia wood; rooted in Wu and Wei EBs

**Requirements met:** (1) Strong Indirect Seal, (2) strong DM, (3) strong Wealth as minister god → "a good chart."

Minister god concept: the supporting element that makes the primary structure function properly. A structural element one tier below the Useful God that enables it.

### M. Ten Days That Transform (化格, 2014)

Ten specific Day Pillars eligible for transformation structures:
Jia Shen, Yi You, Geng Yin, Xin Mao, Ren Wu, Gui Wei, Bing Zi, Ding Chou, Wu Wu, Ji Chou.

- **True transformation (真化)**: produces dukes and high officials (公卿).
- **False / incomplete transformation (假化)**: associated with orphans or adopted status, yet can still achieve notable success.
- "Seeing dragon will change" (见龙则化): encountering Earthly Branch Chen (辰) activates auspicious conditions.

Transformation operates under rules entirely separate from standard Bazi structures. Trey flags interpretive uncertainties rather than rationalizing them.

### N. GuiKang (魁罡) — SMTH (2015)

Four Day Masters: Geng Chen (庚辰), Ren Chen (壬辰), Wu Xu (戊戌), Geng Xu (庚戌).

Chen = TianKang (天罡); Xu = HeGui (河魁) — extinction points of Yin and Yang.

**Characteristics from SMTH:** Intelligent, morally upright, decisive, authoritative. Tendency toward severity (刚烈). "Many GuiKangs gathered → one is extraordinarily successful."

**Critical vulnerability:** Any Wealth or Officer in the chart "causes severe catastrophe." Punishment/clash → "petty people" (小人) outcomes.

**From ZPZQ:** GuiKang is practically superseded by Month Command analysis. Trey estimates fewer than 1% of theoretically qualifying charts actually meet the strict conditions (Month Command genuinely empty, no Wealth/Officer anywhere).

### O. YueLing (月令) Historical Background (2014)

From the dedicated Month Command post:

- **Etymology:** Yue (月) = month; Ling (令) = command. Together: "the entity that takes charge during a certain period of the month."
- **Historical origin:** Recorded in the **Book of Rites (礼记)**, written around 200 BC. The 12 months begin with Yin (寅), marking the start of spring, and end with Chou (丑), closing the year.
- **Primacy:** "Determining the correct Month Command is arguably the most important step in analyzing BaZi." Called "the Key Point" (关键) in almost all classical literature.
- **Focal role:** The entire 格局派 structural method is anchored here — structure, Useful God, purity, and True/False god determination all trace back to correctly identifying what the Month EB commands.

### P. Useful God System — 6 Subcategories (2013 Overview post)

Trey's introductory Useful God post identifies six named subcategories, forming a complete analytical framework:

| Category | Chinese | Function |
|----------|---------|----------|
| 顺用 | Follow Use | Directly use and protect benevolent gods |

### Q. Classical vs Contemporary; WuXing vs ZiPing (2013)

From the Between Classical and Contemporary post:
